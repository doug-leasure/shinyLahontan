Project Overview

The Lahontan Cutthroat Trout Population Simulator forecasts population dynamics and evaluates extinction risks under different management and climate scenarios. The app is powered by a Bayesian MPVA model (Multi-Population Viability Analysis) that was developed as part of NASA's Ecological Forecasting Program. Lahontan cutthroat trout (Oncorhynchus clarkii henshawi) is a federally threatened subspecies of cutthroat trout with dozens of imperiled populations scattered among isolated headwater streams draining into the Great Basin Desert.

The Lahontan MPVA model is driven by a database that was collected over several decades by dedicated people from state and federal agencies, academic institutions, non-profit organizations, and others. The database includes fish counts from over 6,000 electrofishing surveys from 1985 to 2015 at almost 4,000 sites in 155 distinct populations.

Stream conditions for each population were measured annually from 1985 to 2015 using remote sensing, geospatial data, and field surveys to estimate:
stream temperature, stream flow, riparian vegetation condition, and non-native trout densities.

This simulator allows you to forecast population dynamics for each population based on its historical environmental conditions or variations on them.

Update History

Version 1.0 [25 April 2017]
Original release.



Version 1.1 [12 Oct 2017]
Added batch processing capability to the Shiny app.



Version 2.0 [7 November 2017]
Includes a data update from 18 May 2017:
- Updated Lahontan cutthroat trout (LCT) data to include 6641 total observations from 155 populations.
- Updated non-native trout data
- Updated LCT reintroduction data
- Updated site drainage areas

Includes a data update from 15 June 2017:
- Average elevation for each population

Model updates:
- Estimated the effect NDVI separately for high and low elevation populations (i.e. mean elevation above or below 2000m)
- Environmental stochasticity modeled slightly different (Hierarchical half-Cauchy rather than Gamma distribution).
- Sampling model restructured to estimate site abundances.
- Detection probabilities modeled as a deterministic function rather than using Beta regressions with residual observation error.
- MPVA model fit to updated dataset.
